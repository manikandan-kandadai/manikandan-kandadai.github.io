Augmented Human Design

Sci-Fi Design and prototype

|




Challenge

The Task:  To help a client by designing a futuristic product that will address their burning needs and concerns.

Project Type:

Team (Design case competition)

Role:

UX Designer/Prototyper

Tools Used:

Paper Prototyping

Duration:

3 hrs

About the Challenge:  This design challenge is different from the other challenges. Here each team is required to design a futuristic product concept to tackle a real world problem or need. It is okay to go tangential to established status quo in order to design something futuristic.

Discovery

The Client: A women of age 65 who is the caregiver of her husband who has type 2 diabetics

Client Interviews:

The design challenge was organized in a way in which real clients(patients and caregivers) were assigned to each team as resources for us to to work with. The clients ended up being our primary source of information to understand needs of the specific problem we were about to tackle.
The client interviews revealed a lot of information about the greatest fears and motivations of hers and this enabled us to build a psychological profile of the client. This later was used as the persona which enabled us to design a futuristic utopian product that we user tested on the client.

The following were few questions we asked the client:
1) Can you walk us through a typical day of your life?
2) Can you talk about the last time you had difficulty being a care giver?
3) Can you express in your own words what frightens you the most about your husband's health condition?
4) If god grands you a wish to use a technology to solve your problem, which problem do you want to find yourself solving with the wish?



Needs Assessment:

The interview with the client opened up a lot of interesting conversations about their personal life and about technology. For our team, we identified the major pain points of the client to be as follows:
1) Wanted to have an intuitive and easier way to be notified about her husband's health condition
2) Is worried if her kids will get diabetics
3) Wants a way to be assured about quantity of sugar intake by her husband.

Defining the problem:

At this stage, we as a team zeroed in on the problem that we were trying to solve.

To design a sci-fi solution that would notify the client about her husband's insulin level and keeps her husband informed about his current intake of suger.

Design

Design Philosophy: To design the technology and interactions of the future consumer health devices.

Tools: Paper sketching, lo-fi prototyping

Participatory Design:

We started out brainstorming by including the client (who now feels like a designer) and created a journey map of her activities throughout the day. We then identified the painpoints on the map and ideated to address that.

Ideation and Brainstorming

As a team we envisioned seperate digital solutions each for the client and her husband but with varying functionalities. The solution on the client's husband should continually monitor his blood sugar level and provide him a feedback about his current consumption of sweet. The device on the client's end will help her track her husband's health data and would notify her if any anomoly exists.

Prototyping:

We envisioned the device for the client's husband to be a bio-implant that would help him visualize the blood suger level and also access historic data. The implant will also show predictions about his sugar consumption and how it will impact the future.

The digital solution on the client's side will be a wearable bracelet that notifies her if her husband has a sudden anomoly in his blood sugar level and also provides access to his historical data . This notification feature will help the client to contact her doctor if required when she is not around her husband.

User Testing:

The prototype was then handed over to the client to be worn and to get a sense of how it would feel to use the technology. The client was very happy with the design at the end that she requested to keep the prototypes for herself to show it to her grand children.

For us, the user testing in this scenario was not about making it more usable since the design is a sci-fi product but it provided us space to think about the emotions and empathy as a tool to iterate the product over to solve her needs.


Conclusion


The Augmented human challenge was a very unconventional design challenge that made me realize that an ideal design solution should empathise with the target user in addition to solving a user's need.
