Facebook Photos re-design

Design case competition

|




Challenge

The Task:  To modify the photos and album feature of facebook to allow users to search photos and albums easily.

Project Type:

Team (Design case competition)

Role:

Ideator/UX Designer

Tools Used:

Whiteboarding, Sketch, Brainstorming, Paper sketching

Duration:

3 hrs

About the Competition:

FB Design case competition is a Design Jam organised by FB in association with SOCHI Student organisation. It is a limited hour design competition.

Discovery

Target Users: Current users of Facebook who would like to find a specifc picture in their collection of uploaded pictures and albums.

Peer Interviews:

We started the discovery phase by interveiweing our peers and team members to understand how they perform searches to find a specific photo in FB. This obviously doesnt have a breath of target users but we did have people who have never used FB and few users who are addicted to facebook. We interviewd the two kinds of people to understand how they organise photos and how they start searching for a specific photo

Discovery Findings:

The interviews made us all rethink photos not just as digital collection of images but as memories. We realized towards the end of the discovery phase that people have developed a certain kind of mental model while they search for a picture or an album



The Mental Model:
1) People most often search for a picture that has a memory that they cherish.
2) They search for more than one pictures in the same setting (location,type,e.t.c)
3) Users remember a picture by the context unlike a regular phrase search in google. For instance, "I want to see the picture that I took by the pool at Rick's Party last summer."
4) Users also search pictures with whom they have taken it.



Needs Assessment:

The interviews and understanding the user's mental model was very fruitful and showered some light on opportunities that looked promising. It was suprising to see that the existing photos feature of FB doesnt have a higher level of organisation to search for a picture. It was also jarring to find that the user's mental model was not taken into consideration while searching for a picture.



Findings:
1) Users need a place to find pictures they have taken with a specific person
2) Users want to find pictures of their friends taken at the same location/setting
3) Users want to find pictures that had the most social votes or social presence.
4) Users wanted a way to search of specific type of photos across years.

Design

Design Philosophy: To re-design FB photos to make search usable, intuitive and natural to the users.

Tools: Paper sketching, lo-fi prototyping

Whiteboarding:

We took to the whiteboard and started listing out all our findings and started walking through the user experience of a typical user who searches for an image. We explored the possibility of using FB's natural language search for photos. We also wanted the search to be a visual search that the user can recognize in a short span of time. FB's likes and Comment count can be assumed to have the most social memory and hence we wanted to return search results organized by its "social attraction".

Wireframing:

We started wireframing various concepts to test them out. In the process we wanted to make sure that the user's mental model is preserved. For instance, "show me the photos I took at Mark's party" will return a cluster of images that tags to the specifc search keywords. FB seem to have integrated such high level searches on their home screen search bar and hence using those same functionalities seem to be very plausible in the future.

To enhance the UX, we created few mockups that changes the thumbnail size based on the "social attractiveness". We also wanted to display all the search results but making few of them really obvious and larger than others based on the social ranking. For the interaction, we thought about a simple yet intuitive way of gliding one's finger over an array of thumbnails to get a preview (magnified thumbnail) of the picture on which the current finger rests.

Prototyping:

With the limited time left, we wanted to create a medium-high fidelity prototype in sketch that captures few of the important feature we envisioned the final product to contain.

Feedback from FB:

One of UMSI Almuni connected with us throughout the design competition and he provided feedback about the design. He really liked the idea and the concept and felt that the use of User's mental model to design the interactions was a great approach. He did however warn us that the mental model is very subjective of the user's demographics and the cultural background and hence more than just a mental model needs to be taken into account while designing a social platform for all users.


Conclusion


This design competition was one of those design excercisies where you(the designer) is one of the target user but to design something great you need to think beyond yourself as the target user.
This design excersise made me think in terms of emerging markets, different demographics, different mental models based on cultural differences and other user behaviour as a key factor while designing a common social platform.
