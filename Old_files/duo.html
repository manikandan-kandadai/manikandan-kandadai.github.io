Duo Security

Design case competition

_




Challenge

The Task:  To help IT administrators identify employees in an organization who will be affected by phishing scams and report them to executives.

Project Type:

Team (Design case competition)

Role:

UX Designer/Prototyper

Tools Used:

Paper Prototyping, whiteboarding, interaction mapping, Empathy mapping, paper sketching.

Duration:

3 hrs

About the Design Challenge:

This is the Design jam organized by Duo Security along with SOCHI student organisation at UofM. The 3 hour design challenge was focussed around creating a solution to detect and prevent phishing attacks in enterprise.

Discovery

Target User: IT administrators and secrity consultants.

Research and Experience:

We started the Discovery phase by getting to know the problem we are solven a little more in detail as a team. Fortunately, I have worked in Information Security domain earlier and hence had the understanding of what the jargons meant and how the attack vectors worked in real life scenario. I have also used few of the existing security tools and hence I was in a good stand to disseminate the knowledge to me team. Apart from this, we went online and studied the best practices to avoid phishing scams and the best practices to detect employees who are vulnerable. We made use of this research in streamlining our design and approach in the further stages.



Needs Assessment:

Following the research, we started with analysisng the needs of the persona handed over to us by the Duo team.

Persona:
Bio: Mark is an IT Administrator in an organisation and is concerned about the security of his organisation because of the sophestication of hackers and attackers targting all major enterprises. He wants to make sure that he reports quality data to his boss and wants to systematically evaluate the vulnerability of the employees to spear-phishing emails.

Needs as describled in the Persona:
1) A way to identify employees who are vulnerable to phishing scams to avoid any security issues in the organisation. 2) A way to communicate the results to his boss. 3) A way to keep track of the results and perform similar employee vulnerability checks in the future.

Design

Design Philosophy: To design a tool for IT administrators to identify potential risk in an organization.

Tools: Paper sketching, White bording, empathy maps, interaction design.

Using Research Data

We took the research and the evaluated needs to create a basic workflow of decisions we envision the user to make. As a team we came together to understand the various reactions to phishing emails by employees and mapped these actions on a white board. With a clear framework of actions available to us we ideated around this to concentrate on different solutions that can help the IT administrator uniquely identify the percentage of users making specific decisions.

Ideation

While ideating around different solutions within the framework we developed, we also started to test and back our decisions based on the resarch we had. This way we iteratively negated all the assumptions we had while we started out. We also recived postive ffedback from DUO security team whie working on this solution and incorporated some of their feedback while iterating.

Wireframing and Prototyping:

We created a low fidelity prototype of a dashboard targeted towards IT Administrators and Security consultants. The dashbord can be used to lauch new Anti-Phishing campaign with in an organisation and with in specific departments of the organisation. The campaign will send out mass emails which looks legitimate but contains either downloadable files or links to external websites. The details of the employees who are clicking on these links will be captured. To improve the credibility of the campaign, every campaign will be randomnized in the approach and the originating email id. The dashboard will present thes results visually and has graphs that show realtime and historic data. The dashboard also has options to see the Risk index of individual departments. With a click of a button, a report can be generated to show to the higher executives and to make a decision.

Marketing Strategy:

As a part of the solution, we also advocated the a marketing strategy that Duo can choose to implement. We proposed that DUO can bundle this as an addon to their existing security solution or can provide this as a stand alone service. By bundling this to the already existing software, DUO is increasing its product line and consequently increasing its market share.

Feedback from DUO:

We received great feedback from the DUO team and they appreciated our effort and process of integrating the product within their eco system.


Conclusion


Personally, I was very satisfied about the process we as a team went through in this design challenge. I was also very happy that my exisiting knowledge in the domain helped us to narrow down our research on things that matter to the target user.
