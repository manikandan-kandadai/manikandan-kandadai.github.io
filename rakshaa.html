RAKSHAA

An Emergency app like never seen before.

My Design Process

The Problem

Women in developing countries like India have a hard time finding resources in their vicinity which can potentially be a life saver at times of need.


Motivation

On 16 December 2012 in New Delhi, the capital of India, a women was brutally gang raped in a bus and 10 days later she was declared dead. This incident generated widespread national and international coverage and was widely condemned and admonished. The public protested against the government in Delhi for not providing adequate security for women. Similar protests took place throughout the nation asking for stronger reforms in law and order for sexual abuse. 2 years later, the accused were hung to death by the supreme court and several reforms were put to action. There has been considerable increase in crime reported by women and a fall in overall crime against women since then. 

This incident is not uncommon in other developing countries and similar reasons have surfaced for such inhumane act against women. Most women are oblivious to the resources at their disposal and only a design informed by understanding the user’s emotions can solve this burining crisis.

Solution

A mobile application with features informed by user needs and interface evaluated by user enactments.

Digital Discovery and Value Proposition

Market Research

A Market research was performed to identify solutions currently available in the market dealing with women safety. Research was performed on mobile, web and physical products available in the market. 

A SWOT analysis of the various services and features was performed to identify opportunities and features that would make up the value proposition for the application.

Learnings

  • The Competitive Analysis helped me figure out the missed opportunities. 

  • The User Reviews in the App store helped me perform a preliminarily need assessment and emotion coding

  • False positives were a major deterrent for the users to use the application - threats

  • The use of external triggers or notifiers (wearables) did not improve the user experience considerably in this case - Validating assumptions

User Research and Evaluation

Sketching & Brainstorming

With the secondary research and the value proposition, I sketched 8 unique ideas which was later iterated and presented to the target users and other designers to get some feedback on.

The objective of this excersise was to come up with unique solutions to solve the problem without considering technical feasibility or validating any assumptions. This excersise can be thought as a moodboard for further iteration and validation.

PreviousNext

Storyboards & Interaction maps

The 8 concepts where developed further to illustrate a user storyboard. Each story consists of a minimum of 5 sketches/steps adding up to 40 sketches in total. The storyboard is the user journey map illustrating how the users will use the solution. 

The objective of this activity was to identify loopholes in the user interaction and experience for the different ideas.

PreviousNext

Interviews

Inverviews were conducted with target users to understand more about their experience about the problem. The objective for conducting the interview was three-fold. 

  • To understand the needs, emotions and personal experience of the users (victims)

  • To evaluate their current choice of solution.

  • To brainstorm an ideal solution for this problem.

Outcomes

The outcome from the interviews were pretty jaring. I totally wasn’t prepared to expect the answers that I  heard from the interviews.

Here is a summary of the findings from the interviews conducted.

  • Salking occurs all the time as opposed to my perception of the occurance being concentrated at night.

  • Most of the users carry a pepper spray but are afraid to use them because they feel that the situation might escalate or they might be wrong about the person.

  • Most of the mobile applications available on the market are hard to use and have a lot of false triggers.

  • An ideal solution does not exist. Many felt that to completely stop stalking, a combined effort of social responsibility, law enforcement and technology should be in place. 

  • Users felt indifferent towards a wearable or other devices that needs to be charged to be carried around in addition to their mobile devices.

The Value Proposition

The market and user research helped us formulate the value proposition for the solution that solves the problem we identified. The value proposition defined the type of solution, its features and its experience for the users.

To design a personal safety mobile application that encourages social responsibility and is reliable and easy to use.

The Design Phase

Persona

The secondary research followed by interviews helped me map a list of personas who will either be direct or indirect users of the application. 

I also created an anti-persona to be cognizant of the type of users who will not be using this application.

PreviousNext

Brainstorming with users

Now that we have defined the problem statement, identified the target users and their needs and also defined the value proposition, I started to flesh out the ideas I already developed along with the target users.

There is some value to do this activity.

  • The users felt they were a part of designing the application and so the features that they liked and longed for was very apparent in the process

  • Card sorting revealed the organization and hierarchy of the different features in the application.

PreviousNext

Paper Prototyping

If in doubt test! test! test!

The wireframes were completed and a paper prototype was created for hallway testing.

The prototype was tested with 3 target users and 2 general audience to get a holistic view about the interactions and usability. Each participant was given a task to perform without any guidance and were asked to think out loud. The user interactions and the body language was observed and follow-up questions were asked to understand the users mental model

Wireframes & Visual Design

Based on user feedbacks the wireframes were reiterated to address the problems and concerns the users had while navigating the application.
